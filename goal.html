BACKGROUND

During my freshman year at Brown University, I enrolled in CSCI 0130 User Interfaces and User Experiences. This case study is the culmination of my learnings from our first project.

Role
User Research, Sketching, Wireframing, User Testing
Duration
12 weeks
Tools
Figma, Procreate

As an avid to-list creator and bullet-journal user, I was interested in finding out what systems others implement to keep their goals on track. What system could I design to motivate users towards achieving the person that they could become if they were to accomplish their goals?

SOLUTION ✨

I. CONTEXUAL INQUIRY

I began by conducting user interviews where I observed and interviewed three users writing their to-do lists using their system of choice.

Forms of To-Do Lists Observed:
  • Google Doc
  • Google Keep
  • Journal/Personal Notebook
KEY OBSERVATIONS
Approaching the Process

When asked about their goals, all interviewees had an idea of what they wanted to accomplish. However, when asked what system they used to track their progress towards achieving this said goal, there was none.

Motivation

Users expressed needing motivation to achieve their goals and stated that it would be more helpful if they could visualize their goals daily.

II. USER PERSONAS

From my interviews and observations, I developed two extreme user types who write to-do lists.

III. LOW-FIDELITY MOCKUP

Based on my findings from the user interviews, I decided to focus on helping guide users through the process of setting actionable goals. Because I focused on using a to-do list as the interface for my observations, I then was faced with the question: How do I differentiate between a tasks and goals? Everybody knows how to create a to-do list, but they didn’t know how to set a system up for long term goals. Thus, I decided to integrate a to-do list into the app because people agree that to-do lists are part of goal setting.
