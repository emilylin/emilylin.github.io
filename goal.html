BACKGROUND

During my freshman year at Brown University, I enrolled in CSCI 0130 User Interfaces and User Experiences. This case study is the culmination of my learnings from our first project.

As an avid to-list creator and bullet-journal user, I was interested in finding out what systems others implement to keep their goals on track. What system could I design to motivate users towards achieving the person that they could become if they were to accomplish their goals?

I. CONTEXUAL INQUIRY

I began by conducting user interviews where I observed and interviewed three users writing to-do lists using their system of choice.

REFRAMING

Based on my findings from the user interviews, I decided to focus on helping guide users through the process of setting actionable goals. Because I focused on using a to-do list as the interface for my observations, I then was faced with the question: How do I differentiate between tasks and goals? While users were able to create to-do lists, they struggled with setting up a system for managing long-term goals. To address this issue, I integrated a to-do list feature into the app since many people consider to-do lists as a fundamental component of goal-setting.
