During my freshman year at Brown, I enrolled in my first design course: User Interfaces and User Experiences. As part of my first project, I took on the challenge of exploring what systems others implemented to keep their goals on track. As an avid to-list creator and bullet-journal user myself, I wondered: What system could I design to motivate more users towards accomplishing their goals through visualization?

I. USER RESEARCH

I began by conducting user interviews where I observed and interviewed three users writing to-do lists using their system of choice.

üôá Approaching the Process

When asked about their goals, all interviewees had an idea of what they wanted to accomplish. However, when asked what system they used to track their progress towards achieving this said goal, there was none.

üé® Motivation

Users expressed needing motivation to achieve their goals and stated that it would be more helpful if they could visualize their goals daily.

REFRAMING

Based on my findings from the user interviews, I decided to focus on helping guide users through the process of setting actionable goals. Because I focused on using a to-do list as the interface for my observations, I then was faced with the question: How do I differentiate between tasks and goals? While users were able to create to-do lists, they struggled with setting up a system for managing long-term goals. To address this issue, I integrated a to-do list feature into the app since many people consider to-do lists as a fundamental component of goal-setting.

III. LOW FIDELITY MOCKUP

I then began an iterative design process flow where I sketched ideas of the interface and created low-fidelity wireframes.

CORE FUNCTIONALITY

As determined by the results of my user research, the core functionality of the app should encompass the following: (1)The ability to create tasks. (2) Breaking down goals into manageable steps. (3) Crafting a vision board.

üéä Increasing Satisfaction

To increase productivity and motivation, I opted to replace the progress bar on the Dashboard and Goals screen with individual progress bars for each task. One of my classmates expressed that they found it satisfying to accomplish several smaller goals throughout the day, rather than attempting to complete everything in one sitting ‚Äî a feat that is not always possible.
